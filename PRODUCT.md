# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Chicken is for people who keep their own PDFs, EPUBs, and long-form documents on their Mac and want one calm place to discover, organize, and read them. They may be students, researchers, writers, professionals, or heavy readers with scattered files across Downloads, Documents, Desktop, iCloud Drive, and book folders.

## Product Purpose

Chicken is a local-first macOS reading library. It scans user-approved folders for books and documents, helps users review and import what it finds, then gives them a focused reader for PDFs, EPUBs, and supported document formats. Success means users can open the app, find their existing books without manual busywork, read comfortably for long sessions, and keep progress, bookmarks, highlights, and preferences locally without needing an account.

## Brand Personality

quiet, private, scholarly

Chicken should feel like a thoughtful desktop tool: composed, trustworthy, and built for reading rather than selling. The app should respect the user's files and attention. It should feel native to macOS without becoming generic.

## Anti-references

Do not make Chicken feel like a SaaS dashboard, a Kindle storefront, a loud AI reader, or a glassy toy macOS demo. Avoid cluttered merchandising, heavy gradients, fake analytics panels, noisy gamification, and decorative effects that compete with text.

## Design Principles

1. Ask before looking. File discovery should be explicit, scoped, and easy to understand.
2. Review before import. The app should help users sort what it finds instead of dumping every document into the library.
3. Reading is the center. Navigation, metadata, and controls should support long-session focus.
4. Local feels trustworthy. Progress, bookmarks, highlights, and library state should make clear they live on the device.
5. Earn density carefully. Library screens can be efficient, but reader surfaces should breathe.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Chicken should support long reading sessions with legible type, strong contrast, keyboard-friendly navigation, visible focus states, and reduced-motion-safe interactions. Color should never be the only way to communicate state.
